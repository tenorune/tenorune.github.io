A seven-year-old child held in an ICE family detention facility in Texas for seventy days wrote, in a letter to the outside, "I don't want to be in this place I want to go to my school." That letter and others reached ProPublica, which published a story drawing on the children's correspondence.

The day the story published, the facility was raided by federal personnel to confiscate further letters from the children. The administrative response to the publication was not investigation of the conditions described, nor accommodation of the children's stated wishes, but the elimination of the specific medium that had let those statements escape.
