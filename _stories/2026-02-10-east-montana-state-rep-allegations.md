Texas State Representative Ana-María Rodríguez Ramos, posting on BlueSky in February 2026, said her office had received "numerous credible reports of torture, killing, and inhumane treatment of detained individuals at the Camp East Montana migrant detention facility, located within Fort Bliss." She called for an independent investigation and for public hearings by the Texas House Committee on Homeland Security, Public Safety, and Veterans Affairs into conditions at the facility.

A state legislator framing detention-facility allegations in those exact words — torture, killing — and asking for sworn hearings is a different category of evidence than activist reporting or anonymous accounts. Rodríguez Ramos is a sitting officeholder; her statement enters the public record under her name and creates an obligation, at least rhetorically, on the relevant committee chairs to either grant or refuse hearings. Whether the hearings happen is downstream; the statement itself, attributed and on the record, is the news.
