In late February 2026, Inquirer columnist Will Bunch posted a brief notice that a Rohingya refugee whose case he had been following had been "found." The follow-up sentence: "He died." Bunch then turned the case into a column, published in the Philadelphia Inquirer and titled, by his own naming, "A Rohingya refugee wanted freedom. America left him for dead in frigid Buffalo." The Investigative Post in western New York reported the death in parallel.

The Rohingya are a stateless Muslim minority who fled genocidal violence in Myanmar; the United States, under previous administrations, had granted protection to many Rohingya as refugees. The man who died had crossed many borders to arrive at the country that, under earlier policy, would have admitted him. The current sequence — refugee, ICE custody, frigid Buffalo, dead — replaces that arc with a different one. Bunch's framing ("Another murder by our immigration goon squads") is an opinion column's framing; the mortality fact is not.
