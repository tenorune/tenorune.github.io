The New York Times reported in April 2026 that the Trump administration is negotiating with the Democratic Republic of Congo to receive Afghans who worked alongside U.S. forces during the war in Afghanistan and were evacuated to Qatar in the 2021 airlift. According to a U.S. aid worker quoted in the reporting, the choice being presented to those Afghans is the DRC or return to a life under the Taliban — the regime they had risked their lives helping the United States to fight.

The third-country relocation framework is the same one being applied to deportees from inside the United States, scaled to the population of overseas U.S.-allied Afghans the administration is unwilling to admit. The negotiation is with a state that did not host these people, did not promise them anything, and shares no obvious interest in receiving them; what's being constructed is a placement market, where the United States offers concessions in exchange for the recipient country's willingness to absorb a population it has no prior relationship to. The Afghans themselves are not parties to the negotiation.
