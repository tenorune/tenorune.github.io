In October 2025, at the height of Operation Midway Blitz in Chicago, Border Patrol Agent Charles Exum shot Marimar Martínez through her windshield in Brighton Park. The federal account described it as a self-defense shooting after Martínez allegedly tried to run an agent over. Body-camera footage, texts, and emails released in February 2026 — pulled into the public record after a judge lifted a protective order — describe a different scene around the shooting itself and around the institutional reaction to it. Seconds before the collision, an agent on the bodycam audio says, "It's time to get aggressive and get the fuck out." The agents' Tahoe then jerks toward Martínez's car. The collision is captured, then five rapid gunshots.

By 3:11 p.m. that same afternoon — with Martínez still hospitalized and not yet charged — Border Patrol Cmdr. Gregory Bovino, the operation's public face at the time, emailed Exum personally to praise him and offer to extend his retirement past age fifty-seven: "In light of your excellent service in Chicago, you have much left to do!!" In Exum's "Posse Chat" group with fellow agents, the response was congratulatory. One agent wrote, "You are a legend among agents you better fucking know that. Beers on me when I see you at training." When Exum shared a news article headlined "Federal agents taunted Chicago woman to 'do something' before shooting her, attorney claims," he annotated the link with "LMAO." A family member texted that Governor Pritzker was criticizing him publicly. Exum replied: "Awe, I cry."
